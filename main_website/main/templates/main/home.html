Topic Modelling , Keyword Extraction and Graphical Visualization

Topic modelling is a popular technique in natural language processing (NLP) that involves identifying the underlying topics or themes present in a corpus of text. One of the commonly used algorithms for topic modelling is the Rapid Automatic Keyword Extraction (RAKE) algorithm. RAKE is a statistical method that identifies relevant keywords in text by analyzing the frequency and co-occurrence patterns of words.

The RAKE algorithm is implemented in R programming language and can be used for topic modelling in the following steps:

1. Data preprocessing: The first step in topic modelling using the RAKE algorithm is to preprocess the text data. This includes removing stop words, stemming, and converting the text to lowercase.

2. Tokenization: Next, the text data is tokenized into individual words, and their frequency is calculated.

3. Candidate keyword extraction: In this step, candidate keywords are identified by splitting the text into phrases based on punctuation marks and identifying phrases with high word frequency and density.

4. Keyword score calculation: A score is assigned to each candidate keyword based on the frequency and co-occurrence of words in the phrase. The score is calculated by dividing the number of word occurrences in the phrase by the total number of words in the phrase.

5. Topic extraction: The final step in topic modelling using RAKE is to extract the top-ranked keywords from the corpus. These keywords are then used to identify the underlying topics or themes in the corpus.

The RAKE algorithm is known for its simplicity and effectiveness in extracting relevant keywords from text data. However, it has some limitations, such as its inability to capture semantic relationships between words and its dependence on punctuation marks for identifying phrases. Nonetheless, it remains a useful tool for topic modelling and keyword extraction in NLP.
